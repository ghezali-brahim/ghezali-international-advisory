<h2>The UK PE Landscape: Opportunities and Constraints</h2>
<p>The UK mid-market offers abundant opportunities for private equity. Fragmentation, succession-driven exits, and operational underperformance create a steady flow of deal flow. Sectors such as business services, healthcare, technology, and industrials have attracted significant capital. Buy-and-build strategies have proven particularly effective in the UK, where add-on acquisitions can be integrated with relative ease compared to cross-border deals.</p>
<p>At the same time, the UK market presents distinct constraints. Labour shortages have increased wage pressure and made talent retention a priority. Brexit has added complexity to supply chains and regulatory compliance. Inflation and interest rate volatility have heightened the focus on operational improvement and cost discipline. Private equity operational consulting must account for these factors when designing value creation plans.</p>
<p>Fundraising conditions have tightened, and LPs are scrutinising operational capability more closely. GPs that can demonstrate a robust value creation playbook and track record of post-acquisition improvement are better positioned to raise and deploy capital. Our buy and build strategy consulting helps funds articulate and execute that playbook.</p>

<h2>Regulatory and Governance Context</h2>
<p>UK private equity operates within a well-defined regulatory framework. The FCA regulates fund managers, and the Takeover Panel oversees public company acquisitions. For mid-market private transactions, key considerations include competition (CMA), national security (NSI Act), and employment (TUPE). Understanding these frameworks is essential for efficient execution.</p>
<p>Governance expectations have risen. Institutional Limited Partners Association (ILPA) guidelines and ESG considerations are influencing fund terms and portfolio company practices. Our private equity operational improvement work integrates governance and reporting from day one, ensuring alignment with LP expectations and facilitating future exits.</p>
<p>Tax structuring remains important. The UK has specific rules for carried interest, management fees, and deal structures. Working with experienced tax advisers, we help funds optimise structures while maintaining operational flexibility. Cross-border structures for international investors require careful coordination with local counsel.</p>
